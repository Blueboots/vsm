Voice Search Marketing

Is your marketing strategy ready for voice search? Recent research found that only 4 percent of U.S. businesses with a physical location are prepared for voice search. From different search intent to local SEO, there’s a lot to think about when optimizing for voice search. But these changes in how people interact with new technologies are an important opportunity for growth in your business. If you haven’t invested in your voice search marketing strategy yet, now is the time to start. Adopting a voice search marketing strategy isn't just about remaining relevant. It’s also about creating a unique and customized customer experience that will build relationships with your business and lead to brand loyalty. Voice search is on the rise in the marketing world, and will only keep getting bigger. As it does it will change the way SEO is done.

There are specific voice search marketing strategies your business can use to improve your voice search-readiness. For example using keyword phrases that better match voice search queries or an approach to local SEO that responds to the way people make use of a voice assisstant on their mobile devices. If your website content is optimized effectively (particularly for mobile use), your business has a chance to be the first and only result that a digital personal assistant suggests. All this requires a different way of approaching search marketing, and companies may not have the knowledge or resources to dedicate to optimizing their sites and apps for voice search. Luckily there are many digital marketing agencies whose existence relies on keeping up to date with all of the advances in search technology. They have the expertise to develop and implement a voice search marketing strategy for your specific business needs.
